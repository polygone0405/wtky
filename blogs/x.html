Theres a song on the radio in Fallout:New Vegas (NV) that makes me tear up when I listen closely.

Theres multiple songs in NV that make me feel a certain way (heartaches by the number fucked me up after a breakup) but this one is the most special.

The song is called In the Shadow of The Valley

Learning to Accept Death.

After some cursory research I do not know if "Shadow of The Valley" was an intentional allusion to The Bible but that is how I read the lyrics.

The song is a very peaceful track. I would recommend listening to it while reading this!1!

I read the first stanza as introducing the idea that the main character is already accepting of death.

The line "I would like to settle down" especially evokes feelings of being ready.

The second stanza is the one that makes me tear up a bit.

"You have always waited for me.
And you always will be there"

The song talks about the flora after this line but it is this line that hits me. I interpret it as the inevitability of death.

Many pieces of media depict death as a figure who approaches you. My interpretation of this song works with the idea that death waits for you.

Media shows death as something to be feared. That is the human experience isn't it? We want to avoid death?

No. Here death is our friend.

The descriptions of Yosmite valley feel like the afterlife to me. Or perhaps a final resting place?

The repitition of "A little bit futher" in the second and third stanzas makes me feel a strange peace. You are almost dead. You are almost at peace.

Becoming aware of your own mortality the transient nature of everything.
