How to eat for cheap

Recently I have been wanting to get many new things.

New laptop, new desktop, new headphones, new phone etc

My primary expenses are:

  1. Rent
  2. Utilities
  3. Groceries
  4. Leisure

Rent and Utilities can be decreased by living somewhere cheaper and by using less electricity, water etc but I don't really want to do either of those.

What got me thinking is groceries. Specifically food.

What follows is the result of the idea "How do you spend as little on food as possible"

What do you need to eat?

So the cheapest way to spend money on food is by not eating anything at all.

Unfortunately for my savings, Breatharianism is a widely disproven practice.

There are 3 macronutrients:

  1. Carbohydrates
  2. Protein
  3. Fat

The way you breakdown how much you eat each of these depends on your goals.

What is the cheapest source of each macronutrient?

This can be dependent on what your grocery store is like but generally:

Macronutrient Sources
Carbohydrates White rice, rolled oats, whole-wheat pasta, potatos and cabbages
Protien Black beans, dried lentils, chickpeas, canned tuna, canned chicken, eggs*
Fats Vegetable oils, peanut butter, sunflower seeds, butter, canned fatty fish
